🏛️ Key Supreme Court Cases (With Case Numbers)

1. Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab

Case Number: AIR 2005 SC 1821

Year: 2005

Reference: Supreme Court case no. 1821 in All India Reporter 2005

What it says: Hospitals cannot refuse emergency treatment due to insurance or payment disputes. Emergency care must be provided first, payment discussion later.

Which HaqSe topic covers this: Medical/Hospital Claims, Hospital Rights

How to verify:

  • Visit: www.indiankanoon.org
  • Search: "Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab AIR 2005 SC 1821"
  • Or search case number directly
Verify on IndianKanoon

2. Dr. Suresh Gupta v. Government of Delhi

Case Number: AIR 2003 SC 3763

Year: 2003

Reference: Supreme Court case no. 3763 in All India Reporter 2003

What it says: Hospitals cannot hold dead body as security for unpaid bills or pending insurance claims. This violates human dignity and family rights.

Which HaqSe topic covers this: Death Claims, Hospital & Insurance Rights, Body Release Rights

How to verify:

  • Visit: www.indiankanoon.org
  • Search: "Dr. Suresh Gupta v. Government of Delhi AIR 2003 SC 3763"
  • Or visit: Supreme Court website (supremecourtofindia.gov.in)
Verify on IndianKanoon

3. Boliden Nife India Ltd. v. Employees State Insurance Corporation

Case Number: AIR 2001 SC 2089

Year: 2001

Reference: Insurance company cannot use delaying tactics. Claims must be processed within reasonable time.

Which HaqSe topic covers this: Insurance Claims Settlement Timeline

Verify on IndianKanoon

4. National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Jai Prakash

Case Number: AIR 2009 SC 1912

Year: 2009

Reference: Insurance company must pay compensation within reasonable time. Delay can attract penalty.

Which HaqSe topic covers this: Motor Insurance Claims, Claim Settlement

Verify on IndianKanoon

5. Birendra Prasad v. Kamla Devi

Case Number: AIR 2012 SC 2160

Year: 2012

Reference: Pre-existing disease clause cannot be used after 2 years of continuous premium payment.

Which HaqSe topic covers this: Insurance Claims, Pre-existing Disease Denial

Verify on IndianKanoon

⚠️ Important Notes for Verification

1. Case Naming Convention

Indian cases are cited as: [Party Name] v. [Other Party]
Example: "Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab"
"v." means "versus" (against)
Case number follows: AIR [Year] [Court] [Number]

2. AIR Stands For

AIR = All India Reporter
This is the official publication of Indian court judgments
SC = Supreme Court
HC = High Court
Example: AIR 2005 SC 1821 means case published in 2005, Supreme Court

3. How to Cite a Case

Correct Format:
Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab, AIR 2005 SC 1821
OR
Jacob Mathew v. State of Punjab (2005) 5 SCC 1

4. When Supreme Court Changed

Historical Note:
Many old cases cited from 1950s-2000s
Recent changes (2023-2024): New BNS, BSA, BNSS laws
For latest law: Always check current version (2024+)
